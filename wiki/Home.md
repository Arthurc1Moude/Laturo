## Introduction to Laturo

**Laturo** (pronounced: lah-TOO-roh; called "Lature" in English) is a fully independent constructed language (conlang) created using the **Rochopelic Root System** (also known as the Ropelic Spelling System). Unlike many constructed languages that borrow heavily from Latin, Romance languages, or Germanic languages, Laturo is built from the ground up with its own unique linguistic roots and structure.

### What Makes Laturo Unique?

Laturo stands apart from other constructed languages in several key ways:

1. **Complete Independence**: Every word in Laturo is constructed using the Rochopelic Root System, ensuring no direct borrowing from existing natural languages (except for proper names and brand names).

2. **Comprehensive Vocabulary**: With over 10,000 words, Laturo provides vocabulary for:
   - Daily conversations and social interactions
   - Professional and technical fields
   - Academic and scientific discourse
   - Arts, culture, and entertainment
   - Technology and modern life

3. **Systematic Word Formation**: Words are built using a consistent structure:
   - Prefix + Root + Subroot + Suffix + Extra-tail
   - Example: `komputadorelo` (computer) = komput (compute) + ador (agent) + elo (thing)

4. **Rich Grammatical System**: Laturo features:
   - 6 grammatical cases (nominative, accusative, genitive, dative, locative, instrumental)
   - 6 tenses (present, past, future, present perfect, past perfect, future perfect)
   - 4 moods (indicative, subjunctive, imperative, conditional)
   - 3 genders (masculine -o, feminine -a, neutral -e)

5. **Cultural Depth**: Laturo includes cultural norms, such as the respectful capitalization of personal names, which reflects the language's emphasis on social respect and proper communication.

## About the Rochopelic Root System

The **Rochopelic Root System** is the foundation of Laturo's vocabulary. Unlike languages that borrow roots from Latin (like Esperanto) or combine elements from multiple languages (like Interlingua), Rochopelic creates entirely original roots.

### Key Principles:

1. **Phonetic Consistency**: Roots are designed to be pronounceable across different language backgrounds
2. **Semantic Clarity**: Related concepts share similar root structures
3. **Systematic Expansion**: New words can be created following established patterns
4. **No Direct Borrowing**: Maintains linguistic independence

### Example Word Construction:

**tinará** (speaks/is speaking)
- **tin-** = root for "speak/language"
- **-ar-** = verb marker
- **-á** = present tense ending

**tinaró** (spoke)
- Same root **tin-**
- **-ar-** = verb marker  
- **-ó** = past tense ending

## Language Statistics

- **Total Vocabulary**: 10,014 words (and growing)
- **Character Set**: 36 characters (26 basic + 10 extended)
- **Word Structure**: Prefix-Root-Subroot-Suffix-Tail system
- **Grammar Complexity**: Moderate (comparable to Romance languages)
- **Writing System**: Latin alphabet with diacritics

## Example Sentences

Here are some examples to give you a taste of Laturo:

**Basic Greetings:**
```
Saludelo!
Hello!

Komo estas to?
How are you?

Mo estar bono, gračaselo.
I am well, thank you.
```

**Questions:**
```
Varminá do ela vo vim tinará?
How do you speak this language?

Vo esta?
What is this?

Dondelo to estar?
Where are you?
```

**Complex Sentences:**
```
Mo tinará Laturo porkeelo esta bono lenguajelo.
I speak Laturo because it is a good language.

Tor estar aprendizarelo Laturo en internetelo?
Are you learning Laturo on the internet?
```
