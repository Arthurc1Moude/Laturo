# Lesson 5: Basic Questions

Learn to ask and answer all types of questions in Laturo!

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:
- Use all 9 question words correctly
- Ask "what," "who," "when," "where," "why," and "how"
- Form information questions
- Ask yes/no questions
- Answer questions appropriately
- Have question-and-answer conversations

## The Question Words

Laturo has 9 main question words, all starting with **vok-**:

| Laturo | English | Pronunciation | Usage |
|--------|---------|---------------|-------|
| **vo** | what | voh | Simple "what" |
| **vokano** | who | voh-KAH-noh | Person |
| **voketo** | what (thing) | voh-KEH-toh | Object/thing |
| **vokimo** | when | voh-KEE-moh | Time |
| **vokapo** | where | voh-KAH-poh | Place/location |
| **vokuro** | why | voh-KOO-roh | Reason |
| **vokalo** | how | voh-KAH-loh | Manner/way |
| **vokanto** | how much/many | voh-KAHN-toh | Quantity |
| **vokindo** | which one | voh-KEEN-doh | Selection |
| **vokasto** | whose | voh-KAHS-toh | Possession |

### Pattern Recognition

Notice the pattern:
- **vo** = basic "what"
- **vok-** prefix + specific ending = specific question type

## Asking "What" (vo / voketo)

### Simple "What" (vo)

Use **vo** for simple, direct questions:

```
Vo esta?
What is this?

Vo ista?
What is that?

Vo to estar haČerelo?
What are you doing?
```

### "What thing" (voketo)

Use **voketo** when specifically asking about an object:

```
Voketo to estar desearelo?
What (thing) do you want?

Voketo estar en mesa?
What (thing) is on the table?
```

## Asking "Who" (vokano)

Use **vokano** to ask about people:

```
Vokano to estar?
Who are you?

Vokano lo estar?
Who is he?

Vokano estar aquí?
Who is here?

Vokano estar en puerta?
Who is at the door?
```

### Answering "Who" Questions

```
Q: Vokano to estar?
   Who are you?

A: Mo estar Kiro.
   I am Kiro.

Q: Vokano la estar?
   Who is she?

A: La estar Maria, mo amiga.
   She is Maria, my friend.
```

## Asking "Where" (vokapo)

Use **vokapo** to ask about location:

```
Vokapo to estar?
Where are you?

Vokapo estar libro?
Where is the book?

Vokapo mor estar irelo?
Where are we going?

Vokapo lo estar trabajarelo?
Where does he work?
```

### Answering "Where" Questions

```
Q: Vokapo to estar?
   Where are you?

A: Mo estar en casa.
   I am at home.

Q: Vokapo estar libro?
   Where is the book?

A: Estar en mesa.
   It is on the table.
```

## Asking "When" (vokimo)

Use **vokimo** to ask about time:

```
Vokimo to estar llegarelo?
When are you arriving?

Vokimo estar reunionelo?
When is the meeting?

Vokimo mor estar irelo?
When are we going?

Vokimo estar klaselo?
When is the class?
```

### Answering "When" Questions

```
Q: Vokimo to estar llegarelo?
   When are you arriving?

A: Mo estar llegarelo mañanalo.
   I am arriving tomorrow.

Q: Vokimo estar reunionelo?
   When is the meeting?

A: Estar en duvo horaselo.
   It is in two hours.
```

## Asking "Why" (vokuro)

Use **vokuro** to ask about reasons:

```
Vokuro to estar aquí?
Why are you here?

Vokuro lo ne estar?
Why is he not here?

Vokuro mor estar esperarelo?
Why are we waiting?

Vokuro to estar estudiarelo Laturo?
Why are you studying Laturo?
```

### Answering "Why" Questions

Use **porkeelo** (because) to answer:

```
Q: Vokuro to estar aquí?
   Why are you here?

A: Porkeelo mo estar estudiarelo.
   Because I am studying.

Q: Vokuro to estar estudiarelo Laturo?
   Why are you studying Laturo?

A: Porkeelo esta estar interesantelo.
   Because it is interesting.
```

## Asking "How" (vokalo)

Use **vokalo** to ask about manner or method:

```
Vokalo to estar?
How are you?

Vokalo esta estar trabajarelo?
How does this work?

Vokalo mo estar haČerelo esta?
How do I do this?

Vokalo to estar llegarelo aquí?
How did you get here?
```

### Answering "How" Questions

```
Q: Vokalo to estar?
   How are you?

A: Mo estar bono, gračaselo.
   I am well, thank you.

Q: Vokalo to estar llegarelo?
   How did you arrive?

A: Mo estar llegarelo en autobuselo.
   I arrived by bus.
```

## Asking "How Much/Many" (vokanto)

Use **vokanto** to ask about quantity:

```
Vokanto libros to estar?
How many books do you have?

Vokanto kostar esta?
How much does this cost?

Vokanto estudiantes estar aquí?
How many students are here?

Vokanto jaros to estar?
How many years old are you?
```

### Answering "How Much/Many" Questions

```
Q: Vokanto libros to estar?
   How many books do you have?

A: Mo estar triko libros.
   I have three books.

Q: Vokanto kostar esta?
   How much does this cost?

A: Esta kostar dekpenko.
   This costs fifteen.
```

## Asking "Which One" (vokindo)

Use **vokindo** to ask about selection:

```
Vokindo to estar desearelo?
Which one do you want?

Vokindo estar toko libro?
Which one is your book?

Vokindo estar mejorelo?
Which one is better?
```

## Asking "Whose" (vokasto)

Use **vokasto** to ask about possession:

```
Vokasto libro esta estar?
Whose book is this?

Vokasto estar esta?
Whose is this?
```

## Yes/No Questions

### Two Methods

**Method 1:** Rising intonation
```
To estar estudiante?
Are you a student?
```

**Method 2:** Add **vo** at end
```
To estar estudiante vo?
Are you a student?
```

### Answering Yes/No Questions

Always use **ja** (yes) or **ne** (no):

```
Q: To estar feliz?
   Are you happy?

A: Ja, mo estar feliz.
   Yes, I am happy.

A: Ne, mo ne estar feliz.
   No, I am not happy.
```

## Practice Dialogues

### Dialogue 1: Getting Information

```
Person A: Vokano to estar?
          Who are you?

Person B: Mo estar Maria. Yelo to?
          I am Maria. And you?

Person A: Mo estar Kiro. Vokapo to estar trabajarelo?
          I am Kiro. Where do you work?

Person B: Mo estar trabajarelo en escuela.
          I work at school.
```

### Dialogue 2: Making Plans

```
Person A: Vokimo mor estar reunionelo?
          When is our meeting?

Person B: Estar mañanalo en duvo horaselo.
          It is tomorrow at two o'clock.

Person A: Vokapo estar reunionelo?
          Where is the meeting?

Person B: Estar en ofikina.
          It is in the office.
```

### Dialogue 3: Shopping

```
Person A: Vokanto kostar esta?
          How much does this cost?

Person B: Esta kostar dudeko.
          This costs twenty.

Person A: Vokindo estar mejorelo?
          Which one is better?

Person B: Esta estar mejorelo.
          This one is better.
```

### Dialogue 4: Asking About Someone

```
Person A: Vokano la estar?
          Who is she?

Person B: La estar mo hermana.
          She is my sister.

Person A: Vokanto jaros la estar?
          How old is she?

Person B: La estar dudeko uniko jaros.
          She is twenty-one years old.
```

## Common Question Patterns

### Pattern 1: Question Word + Subject + Verb
```
Vokano + to + estar?
Who + you + are?
```

### Pattern 2: Question Word + Verb + Subject
```
Vokapo + estar + libro?
Where + is + book?
```

### Pattern 3: Question Word + Full Sentence
```
Vokimo + to + estar + llegarelo?
When + you + are + arriving?
```
