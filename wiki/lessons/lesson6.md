## Understanding Laturo Verbs

In Laturo, regular verbs follow a simple pattern. Unlike "estar" (which is irregular), most verbs have a consistent structure.

### Verb Structure

Most Laturo verbs end in **-arelo** in their infinitive form:

| Infinitive | English | Pronunciation |
|------------|---------|---------------|
| **hablarelo** | to speak | ah-BLAH-reh-loh |
| **comerelo** | to eat | koh-MEH-reh-loh |
| **vivirelo** | to live | vee-VEER-eh-loh |
| **trabajarelo** | to work | trah-bah-HAR-eh-loh |

### Present Tense Pattern

Good news: Like "estar", regular verbs don't change form in present tense!

```
Subject + Verb + Object
   ↓       ↓       ↓
  Mo  + hablarelo + Laturo
   I  +   speak   + Laturo
```

## Using Verbs in Sentences

### Basic Affirmative Sentences

```
Mo hablarelo Laturo.
I speak Laturo.

To comerelo pan.
You eat bread.

Lo trabajarelo aquí.
He works here.

La estudiarelo medicina.
She studies medicine.

Mor vivirelo en ciudad.
We live in the city.

Lor leerelo libros.
They read books.
```

### With Objects

```
Mo escribirelo carta.
I write a letter.

To beberelo agua.
You drink water.

Lo escucharelo música.
He listens to music.

La amarelo gatos.
She loves cats.
```

### With Location

```
Mo trabajarelo en ofikina.
I work in an office.

To estudiarelo en biblioteca.
You study in the library.

Lor vivirelo en apartamento.
They live in an apartment.
```

## Negative Form

To make verbs negative, add **ne** before the verb:

```
Subject + ne + Verb + Object
```

### Examples

```
Mo ne hablarelo español.
I don't speak Spanish.

To ne comerelo carne.
You don't eat meat.

Lo ne trabajarelo hoyelo.
He doesn't work today.

La ne vivirelo aquí.
She doesn't live here.

Mor ne estudiarelo mañanalo.
We don't study tomorrow.

Lor ne desearelo esta.
They don't want this.
```

### Important Note

Just like with "estar", **ne** always comes BEFORE the verb!

## Asking Questions with Verbs

### Yes/No Questions

Use rising intonation or add **vo** at the end:

```
To hablarelo Laturo?
Do you speak Laturo?

To hablarelo Laturo vo?
Do you speak Laturo?
```

### Answering Yes/No Questions

```
Q: To hablarelo Laturo?
   Do you speak Laturo?

A: Ja, mo hablarelo Laturo.
   Yes, I speak Laturo.

A: Ne, mo ne hablarelo Laturo.
   No, I don't speak Laturo.
```

### Information Questions

Use question words with verbs:

```
Vo to comerelo?
What do you eat?

Vokimo to trabajarelo?
When do you work?

Vokapo lo vivirelo?
Where does he live?

Vokuro la estudiarelo medicina?
Why does she study medicine?

Vokalo mor irelo allá?
How do we go there?
```

## Time Expressions with Verbs

### Time Words

| Laturo | English |
|--------|---------|
| **hoyelo** | today |
| **mañanalo** | tomorrow |
| **ayerelo** | yesterday |
| **ahoralo** | now |
| **siemprelo** | always |
| **nuncalo** | never |
| **a veceselo** | sometimes |
| **cada díalo** | every day |

### Using Time Expressions

Time expressions usually come at the end of the sentence:

```
Mo trabajarelo hoyelo.
I work today.

To estudiarelo mañanalo.
You study tomorrow.

Lo comerelo ahoralo.
He eats now.

La leerelo cada díalo.
She reads every day.

Mor descansarelo a veceselo.
We rest sometimes.

Lor ne trabajarelo nuncalo.
They never work.
```

## Combining Multiple Verbs

Use **yelo** (and) to connect actions:

```
Mo comerelo yelo beberelo.
I eat and drink.

To leerelo yelo escribirelo.
You read and write.

Lo trabajarelo yelo estudiarelo.
He works and studies.

Mor hablarelo yelo escucharelo.
We speak and listen.
```

## Practice Dialogues

### Dialogue 1: Daily Routine

```
Person A: Vo to haČerelo cada díalo?
          What do you do every day?

Person B: Mo despertarelo, comerelo, yelo trabajarelo.
          I wake up, eat, and work.

Person A: Vokimo to trabajarelo?
          When do you work?

Person B: Mo trabajarelo en mañanalo.
          I work in the morning.
```

### Dialogue 2: Languages

```
Person A: To hablarelo Laturo?
          Do you speak Laturo?

Person B: Ja, mo hablarelo Laturo. Yelo to?
          Yes, I speak Laturo. And you?

Person A: Mo estudiarelo Laturo ahoralo.
          I am studying Laturo now.

Person B: Mučelo bono! To leerelo libros en Laturo?
          Very good! Do you read books in Laturo?

Person A: Ne, mo ne leerelo mučalo. Mo estar aprendizarelo.
          No, I don't read much. I am learning.
```

### Dialogue 3: Making Plans

```
Person A: To desearelo irelo en kino mañanalo?
          Do you want to go to the cinema tomorrow?

Person B: Ja! Vokimo mor irelo?
          Yes! When do we go?

Person A: Mor irelo en tardesalo.
          We go in the afternoon.

Person B: Perfektelo! Mo desearelo verelo película.
          Perfect! I want to see a movie.
```

### Dialogue 4: At Work

```
Person A: Vokapo to trabajarelo?
          Where do you work?

Person B: Mo trabajarelo en hospital. Mo estar enfermera.
          I work in a hospital. I am a nurse.

Person A: To trabajarelo cada díalo?
          Do you work every day?

Person B: Ne, mo trabajarelo penko díaselo.
          No, I work five days.
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Wrong:** Mo hablo Laturo. (Spanish conjugation)  
✅ **Correct:** Mo hablarelo Laturo.

❌ **Wrong:** To trabajarelo ne hoyelo. (ne after verb)  
✅ **Correct:** To ne trabajarelo hoyelo.

❌ **Wrong:** Trabajarelo mo en ofikina. (verb before subject)  
✅ **Correct:** Mo trabajarelo en ofikina.

❌ **Wrong:** Mo hablar Laturo. (missing -elo ending)  
✅ **Correct:** Mo hablarelo Laturo.

❌ **Wrong:** Mo estar trabajarelo. (using estar with action verbs incorrectly)  
✅ **Correct:** Mo trabajarelo. (for habitual actions)

## Verb Patterns Summary

### Affirmative Pattern
```
Subject + Verb + (Object) + (Time)
Mo + trabajarelo + en ofikina + hoyelo
```

### Negative Pattern
```
Subject + ne + Verb + (Object) + (Time)
Mo + ne + trabajarelo + en ofikina + hoyelo
```

### Question Pattern
```
Question Word + Subject + Verb + (Object)?
Vokapo + to + trabajarelo?
```
