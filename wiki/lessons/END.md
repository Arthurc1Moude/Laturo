## What You've Accomplished

Over these 6 lessons, you have learned:

### ✅ Lesson 1: First Words and Pronouns
- All 8 personal pronouns (mo, to, lo, la, le, mor, tor, lor)
- Demonstratives (esta, ista, estos, istos)
- Basic sentence structure
- How to introduce yourself

### ✅ Lesson 2: Numbers and Counting
- Numbers 0-1,000,000
- Ordinal numbers (first, second, third...)
- Counting objects and telling quantities
- Asking "how many?"

### ✅ Lesson 3: Greetings and Politeness
- Time-based greetings (morning, afternoon, evening)
- Polite expressions (gračaselo, porfavorelo, diskulpalo)
- How to say goodbye
- Formal vs informal language

### ✅ Lesson 4: The Verb "estar" (to be)
- Present tense conjugation
- Describing states and conditions
- Indicating location
- Forming negative sentences and questions

### ✅ Lesson 5: Basic Questions
- All 9 question words (vo, vokano, vokapo, vokimo, vokuro, vokalo, vokanto, vokindo, vokasto)
- How to ask and answer questions
- Information questions vs yes/no questions

### ✅ Lesson 6: Regular Verbs and Actions
- Regular verb structure (-arelo ending)
- 30+ common action verbs
- Expressing daily activities
- Time expressions with verbs

## What's Next?

Your journey with Laturo doesn't end here - it's just beginning! Here are your next steps:

### 1. Practice, Practice, Practice!

**Daily Practice Ideas:**
- Describe your day in Laturo (5 sentences minimum)
- Count objects around you
- Greet people in Laturo (even if just in your head!)
- Write a short diary entry in Laturo
- Think in Laturo whenever possible

### 2. Review Regularly

**Weekly Review Schedule:**
- Monday: Pronouns and "estar"
- Tuesday: Numbers and counting
- Wednesday: Greetings and politeness
- Thursday: Question words
- Friday: Regular verbs
- Weekend: Practice conversations

### 3. Expand Your Vocabulary

Continue learning new words in these categories:
- Food and drinks
- Colors and descriptions
- Family members
- Weather and seasons
- Hobbies and interests
- Travel and directions

### 4. Advanced Grammar Topics

When you're ready, explore:
- Past tense (talking about what happened)
- Future tense (talking about what will happen)
- Irregular verbs
- Adjectives and adverbs
- Possessive forms
- Prepositions
- Compound sentences

### 5. Join the Community

- Find a language partner to practice with
- Join Laturo discussion groups
- Participate in online forums
- Share your progress and help other learners

### 6. Immerse Yourself

- Read simple Laturo texts and stories
- Listen to Laturo audio (if available)
- Watch videos with Laturo subtitles
- Try to think in Laturo for 5 minutes daily
