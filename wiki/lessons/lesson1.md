# Lesson 1: First Words and Pronouns

Welcome to your first Laturo lesson! By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to introduce yourself and use basic pronouns in simple sentences.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:
- Use all 8 personal pronouns correctly
- Understand singular vs plural forms
- Use demonstratives (this, that)
- Say yes and no
- Form simple sentences with pronouns
- Introduce yourself in Laturo

## Personal Pronouns

Personal pronouns are the foundation of any language. In Laturo, they're simple and logical.

### Singular Pronouns

| Laturo | English | Pronunciation | Usage |
|--------|---------|---------------|-------|
| **mo** | I, me | moh | First person |
| **to** | you | toh | Second person (one person) |
| **lo** | he, him | loh | Third person masculine |
| **la** | she, her | lah | Third person feminine |
| **le** | it | leh | Third person neutral |

### Plural Pronouns

Notice the pattern: add **-r** to make plural!

| Laturo | English | Pronunciation | Pattern |
|--------|---------|---------------|---------|
| **mor** | we, us | mohr | mo + r |
| **tor** | you (plural) | tohr | to + r |
| **lor** | they, them | lohr | lo + r |

### Important Notes

1. **mo, to, lo, la, le** are ALWAYS lowercase (unless starting a sentence)
2. These are basic words that don't follow the full Rochopelic structure
3. **le** is gender-neutral and used for objects or when gender is unknown
4. The plural pattern is consistent: just add **-r**

## Using Pronouns in Sentences

### Subject Position

Pronouns come at the beginning of sentences as subjects:

```
Mo estar aquí.
I am here.

To estar estudiante.
You are a student.

Lo estar profesor.
He is a teacher.

La estar médika.
She is a doctor.

Le estar libro.
It is a book.
```

### Plural Examples

```
Mor estar amigos.
We are friends.

Tor estar estudiantes.
You (all) are students.

Lor estar profesores.
They are teachers.
```

### With Negation

Add **ne** before the verb to make negative:

```
Mo ne estar aquí.
I am not here.

To ne estar estudiante.
You are not a student.

Lor ne estar amigos.
They are not friends.
```

## Demonstratives

These words point to things near or far.

| Laturo | English | Usage |
|--------|---------|-------|
| **esta** | this | Near you |
| **ista** | that | Far from you |
| **estos** | these | Near you (plural) |
| **istos** | those | Far from you (plural) |

### Examples

```
Esta estar libro.
This is a book.

Ista estar mesa.
That is a table.

Estos estar libros.
These are books.

Istos estar mesas.
Those are tables.
```

### Asking "What is this/that?"

```
Vo esta?
What is this?

Vo ista?
What is that?

Vo estos?
What are these?

Vo istos?
What are those?
```

## Yes, No, and Basic Responses

| Laturo | English | Usage |
|--------|---------|-------|
| **ja** | yes | Affirmative |
| **ne** | no | Negative |
| **na** | not | Negation particle |

### Using Yes and No

```
To estar estudiante?
Are you a student?

Ja, mo estar estudiante.
Yes, I am a student.

Ne, mo ne estar estudiante.
No, I am not a student.
```

### More Examples

```
Lo estar aquí?
Is he here?

Ja, lo estar aquí.
Yes, he is here.

Ne, lo ne estar aquí.
No, he is not here.
```

## Practice Dialogues

### Dialogue 1: Meeting Someone

```
Person A: Saludelo! Mo estar Kiro.
          Hello! I am Kiro.

Person B: Saludelo! Mo estar Maria.
          Hello! I am Maria.

Person A: To estar estudiante?
          Are you a student?

Person B: Ja, mo estar estudiante.
          Yes, I am a student.
```

### Dialogue 2: Asking About Things

```
Person A: Vo esta?
          What is this?

Person B: Esta estar libro.
          This is a book.

Person A: Yelo ista?
          And that?

Person B: Ista estar mesa.
          That is a table.
```

### Dialogue 3: Talking About Others

```
Person A: Vokano lo estar?
          Who is he?

Person B: Lo estar mo amigo.
          He is my friend.

Person A: Lor estar estudiantes?
          Are they students?

Person B: Ja, lor estar estudiantes.
          Yes, they are students.
```
