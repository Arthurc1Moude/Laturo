# Lesson 3: Greetings and Politeness

Learn how to greet people, be polite, and have basic social interactions in Laturo!

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:
- Greet people at different times of day
- Say goodbye appropriately
- Use polite expressions (please, thank you, sorry)
- Ask "How are you?" and respond
- Have a basic conversation
- Understand formal vs informal greetings
- Show respect through proper language

## Basic Greetings

### Universal Greeting

| Laturo | English | Pronunciation | When to Use |
|--------|---------|---------------|-------------|
| **Saludelo** | Hello | sah-loo-DEH-loh | Anytime, anywhere |

This is your go-to greeting for any situation!

### Time-Based Greetings

Use these based on the time of day:

| Time | Laturo | English | Pronunciation |
|------|--------|---------|---------------|
| Morning (6 AM - 12 PM) | **Buenoselo diasalo** | Good morning | bweh-noh-SEH-loh dee-AH-sah-loh |
| Afternoon (12 PM - 6 PM) | **Buenaselo tardesalo** | Good afternoon | bweh-nahs-EH-loh tar-DEHS-ah-loh |
| Evening (6 PM - 10 PM) | **Buenaselo nočesalo** | Good evening | bweh-nahs-EH-loh noh-CHEHS-ah-loh |
| Night (10 PM - 6 AM) | **Buenaselo nočesalo** | Good night | bweh-nahs-EH-loh noh-CHEHS-ah-loh |

### Important Cultural Note

In Laturo culture, using the correct time-based greeting shows respect and awareness. Using "Buenoselo diasalo" at 3 PM would be incorrect!

## Saying Goodbye

### Basic Farewells

| Laturo | English | Pronunciation | When to Use |
|--------|---------|---------------|-------------|
| **Adioselo** | Goodbye | ah-dee-OH-seh-loh | Formal, final goodbye |
| **Hastalo luegoalo** | See you later | AHS-tah-loh lweh-GOH-ah-loh | Casual, will see again |
| **Hastalo prontelo** | See you soon | AHS-tah-loh PROHN-teh-loh | Expect to meet soon |

### Examples

```
Adioselo, Señorelo Martinez.
Goodbye, Mr. Martinez.

Hastalo luegoalo, amigo!
See you later, friend!

Hastalo prontelo!
See you soon!
```

## Essential Polite Expressions

These words are crucial for respectful communication:

| Laturo | English | Pronunciation | Usage |
|--------|---------|---------------|-------|
| **Gračaselo** | Thank you | grah-CHAH-seh-loh | Expressing gratitude |
| **Mučaselo gračasalo** | Thank you very much | MOO-chahs-eh-loh grah-CHAH-sah-loh | Strong gratitude |
| **Porfavorelo** | Please | pohr-fah-VOH-reh-loh | Making requests |
| **Diskulpalo** | Excuse me / Sorry | dees-KOOL-pah-loh | Apologizing |
| **Perdonelo** | Pardon / Forgiveness | pehr-DOH-neh-loh | Asking forgiveness |
| **Denadalo** | You're welcome | deh-NAH-dah-loh | Responding to thanks |

### Using Polite Words

```
Porfavorelo, vo esta?
Please, what is this?

Gračaselo por ayudalo.
Thank you for the help.

Diskulpalo, mo estar tardelo.
Sorry, I am late.

Denadalo, ne problemalo.
You're welcome, no problem.
```

## Asking "How Are You?"

### The Question

| Laturo | English | Pronunciation |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| **Komo estas to?** | How are you? | KOH-moh EHS-tahs toh? |

### Common Responses

| Laturo | English | Pronunciation |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| **Bono** | Good | BOH-noh |
| **Muyelo bono** | Very good | MOO-yeh-loh BOH-noh |
| **Bien** | Fine/Well | bee-EHN |
| **Mal** | Bad | mahl |
| **Así así** | So-so | ah-SEE ah-SEE |

### Complete Responses

```
Mo estar bono, gračaselo.
I am good, thank you.

Mo estar muyelo bono, yelo to?
I am very good, and you?

Mo estar bien, gračaselo. Yelo to?
I am fine, thank you. And you?

Ne muyelo bono, pero gračaselo.
Not very good, but thank you.
```

## Basic Conversations

### Conversation 1: Meeting Someone New

```
Person A: Saludelo! Mo estar Kiro.
          Hello! I am Kiro.

Person B: Saludelo, Kiro! Mo estar Maria. Mučelo gustoalo!
          Hello, Kiro! I am Maria. Nice to meet you!

Person A: Igualmentelo! Komo estas to?
          Likewise! How are you?

Person B: Mo estar bono, gračaselo. Yelo to?
          I am good, thank you. And you?

Person A: Mo estar muyelo bono.
          I am very good.
```

### Conversation 2: Morning Greeting

```
Person A: Buenoselo diasalo, Señorelo Martinez!
          Good morning, Mr. Martinez!

Person B: Buenoselo diasalo! Komo estas to hoyelo?
          Good morning! How are you today?

Person A: Mo estar bien, gračaselo. Yelo to?
          I am fine, thank you. And you?

Person B: Muyelo bono, gračaselo.
          Very good, thank you.
```

### Conversation 3: Saying Goodbye

```
Person A: Bueno, mo estar irelo. Hastalo luegoalo!
          Well, I am going. See you later!

Person B: Hastalo luegoalo! Buenaselo tardesalo!
          See you later! Good afternoon!

Person A: Igualmentelo!
          Likewise!
```

### Conversation 4: Apologizing

```
Person A: Diskulpalo, mo estar tardelo.
          Sorry, I am late.

Person B: Ne problemalo. Sentarelo, porfavorelo.
          No problem. Sit down, please.

Person A: Gračaselo por komprendizarelo.
          Thank you for understanding.

Person B: Denadalo.
          You're welcome.
```

## Formal vs Informal

### Informal (Friends, Family)

```
Saludelo!
Hello!

Komo estas?
How are you?

Hastalo luego!
See you later!
```

### Formal (Strangers, Elders, Professionals)

```
Buenoselo diasalo, Señorelo.
Good morning, Sir.

Komo estas to hoyelo?
How are you today?

Adioselo, Señorelo Martinez.
Goodbye, Mr. Martinez.
```

## Cultural Tips

### Important Reminders:

1. **Use time-appropriate greetings**: Shows cultural awareness
2. **Always say "gračaselo"**: Gratitude is highly valued
3. **Use "porfavorelo" for requests**: Politeness is essential
4. **Apologize when late**: "Diskulpalo" shows respect
5. **Capitalize names**: Not doing so is disrespectful!

### Politeness Levels:

- **Casual**: Saludelo, Komo estas?
- **Standard**: Saludelo, Komo estas to?
- **Formal**: Buenoselo diasalo, Señorelo. Komo estas to hoyelo?
