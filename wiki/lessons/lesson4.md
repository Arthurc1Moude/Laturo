# Lesson 4: The Verb "estar" (to be)

Master the most important verb in Laturo - the foundation of all sentences!

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:
- Conjugate "estar" in present tense
- Use "estar" to describe states and conditions
- Use "estar" to indicate location
- Use "estar" to identify things and people
- Form negative sentences with "estar"
- Ask yes/no questions with "estar"
- Understand SVO (Subject-Verb-Object) word order

## What is "estar"?

**Estar** is the verb "to be" in Laturo. It's used to:
- Describe how someone feels or their state
- Tell where someone or something is located
- Identify what something is
- Describe temporary conditions

## Present Tense Conjugation

The good news: **estar** doesn't change form! The pronoun tells you who/what.

| Person | Laturo | English | Pronunciation |
|--------|--------|---------|---------------|
| mo | **mo estar** | I am | moh ehs-TAHR |
| to | **to estar** | you are | toh ehs-TAHR |
| lo | **lo estar** | he is | loh ehs-TAHR |
| la | **la estar** | she is | lah ehs-TAHR |
| le | **le estar** | it is | leh ehs-TAHR |
| mor | **mor estar** | we are | mohr ehs-TAHR |
| tor | **tor estar** | you (plural) are | tohr ehs-TAHR |
| lor | **lor estar** | they are | lohr ehs-TAHR |

### Key Point

Unlike many languages, **estar** stays the same for all persons. Only the pronoun changes!

## Sentence Structure: SVO

Laturo follows **Subject-Verb-Object** word order (like English):

```
Subject + Verb + Object/Adjective
   ↓       ↓         ↓
  Mo  +  estar  +  bono
   I   +   am    +  well
```

## Using "estar" to Describe States

### Feelings and Emotions

| Laturo | English |
|--------|---------|
| feliz | happy |
| triste | sad |
| cansado | tired |
| enojado | angry |
| nervioso | nervous |
| tranquilo | calm |

### Examples

```
Mo estar feliz.
I am happy.

To estar cansado.
```

## Negative Form

To make a sentence negative, add **ne** before **estar**:

```
Subject + ne + estar + Object
```

### Examples

```
Mo ne estar feliz.
I am not happy.

To ne estar aquí.
You are not here.

Lo ne estar estudiante.
He is not a student.

La ne estar en casa.
She is not at home.

Mor ne estar cansados.
We are not tired.

Lor ne estar profesores.
They are not teachers.
```

### Important Note

**ne** always comes BEFORE the verb!

## Asking Questions

### Yes/No Questions

Two ways to ask yes/no questions:

**Method 1:** Use rising intonation (spoken)
```
To estar estudiante?
Are you a student?
```

**Method 2:** Add **vo** at the end
```
To estar estudiante vo?
Are you a student?
```

### Answering Yes/No Questions

```
Question: To estar feliz?
          Are you happy?

Answer (Yes): Ja, mo estar feliz.
              Yes, I am happy.

Answer (No): Ne, mo ne estar feliz.
             No, I am not happy.
```

### More Examples

```
Q: Lo estar aquí?
   Is he here?

A: Ja, lo estar aquí.
   Yes, he is here.

Q: Lor estar estudiantes?
   Are they students?

A: Ne, lor ne estar estudiantes.
   No, they are not students.
```

## Practice Dialogues

### Dialogue 1: Meeting Someone

```
Person A: Saludelo! Mo estar Kiro. To estar estudiante?
          Hello! I am Kiro. Are you a student?

Person B: Ja, mo estar estudiante. Yelo to?
          Yes, I am a student. And you?

Person A: Mo estar profesor.
          I am a teacher.

Person B: Mučelo gustoalo!
          Nice to meet you!
```

### Dialogue 2: Asking About Location

```
Person A: Vokapo Maria estar?
          Where is Maria?

Person B: La estar en casa.
          She is at home.

Person A: Yelo Juan?
          And Juan?

Person B: Lo estar en escuela.
          He is at school.
```

### Dialogue 3: Describing States

```
Person A: Komo estas to?
          How are you?

Person B: Mo estar cansado. Yelo to?
          I am tired. And you?

Person A: Mo estar bono, gračaselo.
          I am well, thank you.
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Wrong:** Mo es feliz. (using "es" instead of "estar")  
✅ **Correct:** Mo estar feliz.

❌ **Wrong:** To estar ne aquí. (ne after estar)  
✅ **Correct:** To ne estar aquí.

❌ **Wrong:** Estar mo feliz. (verb before subject)  
✅ **Correct:** Mo estar feliz.

❌ **Wrong:** Mo estoy feliz. (Spanish conjugation)  
✅ **Correct:** Mo estar feliz.
